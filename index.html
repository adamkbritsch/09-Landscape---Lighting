Design stories

How we plan contrast, hierarchy, and alignment

Every project receives a documented story for plant palettes, lighting levels, and circulation so homeowners know exactly why each choice matters.

Contrast mapping

Layer primary focal points at 35–40 lumens and support them with 15–20 lumen fills so paths stay legible without overpowering the planting beds.

Wayfinding cues

Group path lights in pairs, align them with existing hardscape joints, and aim bollards away from neighboring windows to eliminate glare.

Visual tangents

We review every sightline at seated and standing heights to prevent fixtures, tree canopies, and architectural features from merging unintentionally.

Process framework

How we move from sketch to glow

  1. Discovery. Walk the property, study utilities, and document how the space is used after dark.
  2. Design. Deliver lighting levels, planting palettes, and wayfinding diagrams for each zone.
  3. Install & care. Coordinate trades, aim fixtures, and maintain lamps and plantings seasonally.
